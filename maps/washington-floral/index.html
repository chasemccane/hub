01 — Your Business Today

A strong wholesale operation
at a critical inflection point.

Washington Floral has built a respected wholesale floral business across three markets — Tacoma, Spokane, and Boise. The challenge isn't the business. It's the platform holding it together, and the clock is running.

Core Business

Wholesale First

The majority of WFS revenue is B2B — florists, shops, and commercial buyers served by dedicated sales reps across three locations. That relationship-driven model needs modern tooling, not just a website.

Current Stack

Industry System + QuickBooks

A floral-specific front-end sits on top of QuickBooks, handling sales orders, purchase orders, and sales reporting. It works — but it's being discontinued, and the clock is ticking on finding a replacement.

Online Today

10–15% Online, Two Channels

An outdated portal through the current system handles in-stock sales. A third-party marketplace handles vendor-connected inventory. Neither is purpose-built for the WFS buying experience.

The Hard Part

Advance Orders Aren't Supported

Flowers are sold in advance — before inventory is confirmed, before final pricing is set. The current system can't take orders for products that aren't yet in stock. That gap costs you sales every season.

Where the Real Gaps Are
No Advance Ordering
Wholesale floral sales are forward-looking by nature. A platform that can only sell what's in stock today isn't built for how your business actually works.
Rep Order Entry Is Manual
Orders from commercial customers flow through phone, email, and text to assigned sales reps. There's no central system tying rep activity to accounts, commissions, or history.
Outdated Customer Portal
The current online portal is built on aging technology. It wasn't designed to grow — and it shows. Your wholesale buyers deserve a modern self-service experience.
No Unified View Across Locations
Three locations operating independently means no shared customer data, inventory visibility, or reporting across Tacoma, Spokane, and Boise.
Urgent
The current platform is being discontinued by its parent company. This is a forced transition — but handled well, it's an opportunity to build a significantly better foundation. The goal is to make the right decision once, not scramble to a stopgap.

02 — Near-Term Requirements

Solving the immediate challenge.
Without compromise.

Your near-term need is clear: replace a discontinued system with something that handles the complexity of wholesale floral — advance orders, rep order entry, flexible payments, and multiple buyer types. Shopify Plus is built for exactly this.

📋
Advance Orders via Draft Orders
Sales reps can create draft orders for items not yet in stock — with placeholder pricing that gets confirmed when inventory and final costs are known. No more lost seasonal sales.
👤
Sales Rep Order Entry
Reps can build and submit orders on behalf of any customer directly in Shopify. Every order is tracked against the account, keeping activity visible and commissions attributable.
🔔
Rep Notifications on Major Orders
When a commercial customer places a significant self-service order, the assigned rep is automatically notified — so the personal relationship stays intact even as ordering moves online.
🏢
Company Accounts with Buyer Roles
Set up individual company profiles for every wholesale account. Multiple buyers per company, each with their own login — but a single account record tied to their sales rep and pricing tier.
💲
Custom Pricing Per Account
Different florists, shops, and commercial buyers get different pricing — managed natively in Shopify without workarounds or manual overrides. The buyer logs in and sees their rate automatically.
📅
Net Payment Terms Built In
Net 30 and other payment terms are native to Shopify B2B checkout. Wholesale customers purchase now, pay later — with invoice tracking and reminders built into the platform.
🌿
Pre-Order Apps for Online Buyers
For customers ordering through the self-service portal, pre-order apps allow advance purchases against confirmed seasonal inventory — solving the forward-order gap for online channels.
🔐
Gated B2B Storefront
Wholesale buyers log in to see their pricing. Retail customers see standard rates. The same storefront handles both audiences — separated by login, not by separate systems.
📦
Quick Reorder from History
Buyers can reorder from past purchases in seconds — reducing friction on the standing orders and recurring purchases that make up the bulk of wholesale floral revenue.
The Sales Rep Relationship

One of WFS's core strengths is the personal relationship between sales reps and commercial accounts. Shopify is designed to preserve and enhance this — not replace it. Reps get a proper tool for order entry and account management. Customers get a modern self-service option that always keeps the rep in the loop.

05 — Payment Flexibility

The payment setup
that works for WFS.

Wholesale floral has real payment complexity — advance orders with uncertain pricing, Net 30 accounts, ACH collection, and retail customers paying in full upfront. Shopify handles all of it natively, with full flexibility on how you process payments.

📄
Net 30 Terms — Native
B2B wholesale accounts can purchase online and pay later. Net 30, Net 60 — whatever terms you set per account. Invoice tracking, payment reminders, and status are all managed in-platform.
🏦
ACH Payments Supported
ACH is a native payment method in Shopify. Wholesale customers on ACH don't need a workaround — it's a first-class option at checkout alongside credit and debit cards.
💳
Credit & Debit for Retail
Retail customers pay in advance by card — whether online, via the self-serve portal, or in-store through POS. The same payment infrastructure handles all channels seamlessly.
🔄
Keep Your Existing Merchant
If WFS has pre-negotiated card processing rates, you can continue using that merchant. Shopify supports external payment gateways. On Plus, the platform transaction fee is just 0.20% — typically well below the savings from an existing negotiated rate.
⚡
Or Use Shopify Payments
Shopify Payments is Shopify's built-in processor — competitive rates, zero Shopify transaction fees, and full integration with the admin. Credit card, ACH, and digital wallets are all included with no third-party setup.
📋
Draft Order Payments — Finalized Later
For advance orders placed before pricing is confirmed, draft orders can be created and sent as invoices. The buyer pays when pricing is finalized — no system-level conflict with uncertain inventory or costs.
Payment Complexity is Solved, Not Worked Around

Wholesale floral has always had payment complexity that generic e-commerce platforms weren't built to handle. Advance orders. Net terms. ACH collection. Mixed buyer types. Shopify Plus handles all of it natively — not through third-party patches or manual processes bolted on after the fact. This is built for how WFS actually operates.
