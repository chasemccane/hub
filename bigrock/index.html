Where Things Stand Today

Built on workarounds. Ready for something better.

Big Rock Supply has operated for over a century on hard work and deep customer relationships. The tools haven't kept pace — and the cracks are showing across every channel.

📞

Contractors Call to Get Their Price

Every B2B customer with a negotiated rate has to call a sales rep to place an order. There's no self-serve portal, no automated pricing — just phone calls that tie up staff and create friction at scale.

🔀

Three Systems, Zero Visibility

DTC orders live in Cart.com. B2B is handled manually through BC365. The Chicago store runs separately. There's no unified view of a customer, no cross-channel reporting, and no single source of truth.

💳

Authorize.net Bleeding Margin

Using a third-party gateway on Shopify triggers an additional transaction fee on every order — on top of Authorize.net's own processing rates. Every sale costs more than it needs to.

🚚

Freight Quotes Are a Black Box

Roofing and construction supply ships heavy. Without real-time LTL/freight quoting at checkout, customers either see inaccurate rates or have to wait for a rep to call back with a shipping number.

🏪

Chicago Store Is an Island

The Chicago physical location operates disconnected from the online business. There's no shared inventory visibility, no unified customer record, and no way to attribute in-store activity to the broader account relationship.

🗄️

BC365 Data Stuck in a Silo

Business Central holds the source of truth for products, pricing, and inventory — but that data doesn't flow cleanly to the front-end commerce experience. Changes in BC require manual re-entry elsewhere.

📊

No Unified Analytics or Reporting

With three disconnected systems, there's no way to see total customer lifetime value, channel performance, or product trends across B2B and DTC in one place. Business intelligence requires manual aggregation.

👔

Outside Reps Can't Place Orders for Customers

Field reps and sales staff have no clean way to enter orders on behalf of contractor accounts. The workflow is manual, error-prone, and relies on back-office staff to key in what reps relay over the phone.

The Core Philosophy

Shopify owns the commerce.
Business Central owns the books.

The question isn't whether to keep Business Central — it's about where commerce logic lives. BC365 was built to be a world-class ERP. It was not built to power a B2B self-serve portal, a DTC storefront, or a retail POS floor.

When an ERP takes on commerce operations, the result is manual processes, fragile integrations, and a degraded customer experience — because it wasn't designed for that job.

The Shopify approach is the inverse: Shopify handles every customer touchpoint — contractor portal, DTC, in-store POS, outside sales orders — and passes clean, reliable data back to BC365. BC does what it's great at: financial management, inventory, and ERP.

✕

ERP-led commerce: Manual order entry in BC, no self-serve portal, pricing updates require IT, customer experience is an afterthought

✓

Shopify-led commerce: Contractors log into their own portal, pricing flows from BC automatically, every order syncs back to BC as a financial record

✓

Pre-built BC365 connector: Microsoft-certified, off-the-shelf, bidirectional sync — no custom integration project required

✓

One unified admin: DTC, B2B portal, Chicago POS, and outside sales orders all live in a single Shopify dashboard with unified reporting
