Where Things Stand Today

Built on workarounds. Ready for something better.

The team at Southern Concrete has done a heroic job making disconnected systems function — but the foundation is showing its age.

🔧

POS That Wasn't Built for Retail

The readymix ERP was configured to act as a POS. Staff open and close the full ERP system to process each transaction — slow, manual, and not designed for retail floor use.

📋

Manual Inventory Every Month

Inventory management is entirely manual. No real-time visibility across the 3 store locations. The goal of this project is to put those habits behind you for good.

👤

COD Buyers Are Invisible

Trucks roll up at the quarry, pick up concrete, and pay by credit card — but there's no way to capture who they are. Repeat buyers remain unknown. Relationship-building is impossible.

🏷️

Contractor Pricing via Coupons

Customers with negotiated contractor rates are handled through manual coupon discounts — a fragile, error-prone process that creates guesswork at the register.

💳

Multiple Payment Processors

Retail stores and quarry COD sales each use separate credit card systems. Reconciliation is complex, rates aren't optimized, and there's no unified view of payment activity.

🔄

NetSuite Implementation at Risk

The parallel NetSuite rollout cannot advance until a POS decision is made. The clock is ticking — the legacy ERP reaches end-of-life at year's end.

The Core Philosophy

Shopify owns the commerce.
NetSuite owns the books.

The debate isn't whether to use NetSuite — it's about how much commerce logic to push into NetSuite versus keeping it where it belongs: in a purpose-built commerce platform.

When NetSuite takes on 50–60% of your payment processing and POS operations, you're asking an accounting system to do a retail job. The result is complexity, manual workarounds, and brittle integrations.

The Shopify approach is the inverse: Shopify handles every customer touchpoint — in-store, B2B, COD, field orders — and passes clean, reliable data to NetSuite as cash sales. NetSuite does what it's great at: accounting, reporting, and ERP.

✕

NetSuite-led processing: ERP manages the POS experience, staff open accounting software at the register, custom integrations break when NetSuite updates

✓

Shopify-led commerce: Staff use a purpose-built iPad POS, every transaction flows automatically to NetSuite as a cash sale, inventory is consumed in real time

✓

Pre-built connector: No custom development needed. Shopify ↔ NetSuite sync is off-the-shelf, bidirectional, and battle-tested

✓

One source of truth: Pricing, customers, inventory, and orders all live in Shopify — NetSuite inherits the data it needs automatically

NetSuite Integration

The integration story is already written.

Shopify has a pre-built, bidirectional NetSuite connector. No custom development, no bespoke ERP work — just configuration and go-live.

1

NetSuite owns the price lists

Contractor catalogs, tiered pricing, and product definitions live in NetSuite. Shopify pulls them automatically — no manual updates at the register.

2

Shopify owns the transaction

Every sale — POS, B2B, COD, or draft order — is processed through Shopify. Staff never touch NetSuite on the floor. The POS just works.

3

NetSuite receives a clean cash sale

Each transaction flows into NetSuite as a cash sale. Money is captured immediately. Inventory is consumed. No lag, no manual reconciliation, no separate fulfillment step.

4

Inventory stays in sync — automatically

Products, SKUs, and stock levels move bidirectionally. NetSuite manages warehouse transfers and purchase orders. Shopify reflects real-time availability across all store locations.
